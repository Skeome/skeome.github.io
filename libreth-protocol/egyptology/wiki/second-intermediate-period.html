Overview of Fragmentation

The Second Intermediate Period is often simplified as the era of Hyksos rule, but it was in fact a complex period of political fragmentation in which Egypt was divided into at least five concurrent powers. This era followed the collapse of the Middle Kingdom's centralized authority.

The Hyksos (15th Dynasty)

Rulers of Levantine origin who controlled the Nile Delta from their capital at Avaris. They introduced the chariot and advanced metal-working to Egypt.

The Theban 17th Dynasty

Indigenous Egyptian rulers based in Upper Egypt (Thebes). They eventually launched the war of liberation that led to the New Kingdom.

The Kerma Kingdom (Nubia)

A powerful, non-Egyptian kingdom to the south that exerted significant pressure on Egypt's borders and occasionally allied with the Hyksos.

The Abydos Dynasty

A short-lived, independent local dynasty that controlled the sacred region of Abydos during the period of maximum fragmentation.

The War of Liberation

The conflict between the Thebans and the Hyksos reached its peak under the final rulers of the 17th Dynasty: Seqenenre Tao, Kamose, and Ahmose I. Seqenenre Tao likely died in battle against the Hyksos, as evidenced by his heavily traumatized mummy. His son Ahmose I eventually succeeded in expelling the Hyksos and reunifying Egypt, founding the 18th Dynasty and the New Kingdom.
