Thoth [Djehuty]: Lord of the Sacred Words

The Egyptian deity Thoth (Djehuty) was the lord of writing, wisdom, magic, the moon, and knowledge. His primary cult center was at Hermopolis (Khemenu). He bore titles such as "The Silver Aten" (representing the moon as the night-sun) and "He Who Balances the Ship" (referring to Ma'at).

Mythic Role: The Great Mediator

In the Osiris myth, Thoth is the advocate who defends Osiris before the tribunal. He is also the healer who restores the Eye of Horus (Wedjat) after Set tears it out, using his spit (magic) to make it whole again. He is the cosmic mathematician who won the five epagomenal days from the moon to allow Nut to give birth to the gods.

The Books of Thoth & Hermes

The tradition of Thoth as the author of all sacred books is ancient. In the Demotic Book of Thoth, a dialogue takes place between "The One who Loves Knowledge" (the aspirant) and "The One who Praises Knowledge" (Thoth), detailing the initiation into the scribal craft.

The 42 Books of Hermes

Clement of Alexandria (c. 200 CE) described a procession of priests carrying the 42 fundamental books of Egyptian wisdom, attributed to Hermes. These included: 10 on the gods, 10 on ritual, 4 on astronomy, 2 on music, and 6 on medicine.

Artapanus & Syncretism

The historian Artapanus (2nd century BCE) went even further, identifying Thoth-Hermes with the biblical Moses, claiming that Moses taught the Egyptians philosophy and was named "Hermes" by the priests for his interpretation of sacred texts.

The Evolution to Trismegistus

The epithet "Trismegistus" (thrice-greatest) is rooted in an Egyptian linguistic convention found as early as 172 BCE, where Thoth was described as "the greatest and greatest god, great Hermes". This title reflected his status as the premier philosopher, priest, and king.

Through the process of syncretism in Alexandria, Thoth was merged with the Greek Hermes. Both deities shared attributes as inventors of writing, patrons of intellectual activity, and psychopomps who guided souls between realms.

Patron of the House of Life

Thoth was the patron deity of the House of Life (Per Ankh), where the sacred scrolls and magical documents were managed. He was believed to have revealed all knowledge of astronomy, geometry, architecture, and medicine to humanity.
