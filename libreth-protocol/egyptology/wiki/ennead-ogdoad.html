The Great Ennead (Heliopolis)

The Ennead (from Greek ennéas, "nine") was a group of nine deities worshipped at Heliopolis (Ancient Egyptian Iunu). It represents the generation of physical reality from a single source.

Atum

The Primordial Monad who brought himself into being from Nun.

Shu & Tefnut

Air and Moisture. The first duality emerged from the One.

Geb & Nut

Earth and Sky. The foundation of the physical world.

The Ennead is completed by the four children of Geb and Nut: Osiris, Isis, Set, and Nephthys, who represent the introduction of human society and the moral polarities of existence.

The Ogdoad (Hermopolis)

The Ogdoad (Greek ogdoás, "eight") was a system of eight primordial deities worshipped at Hermopolis (Ancient Egyptian Khemenu, "City of the Eight"). These four pairs of male (frog-headed) and female (snake-headed) deities represent the pre-creation state of the universe:

Nu & Naunet

The Primordial Waters (Depth).

Heh & Hauhet

Infinite Space (Infinity).

Kek & Kauket

Primordial Darkness.

Amun & Amaunet

Hiddenness (The Hidden Spirit).

The Ogdoad eventually merged to create the "Cosmic Egg" from which the sun god emerged, signaling the start of time.
