The Great Ennead (Heliopolis)

The Ennead (from Greek ennéas, "nine") represents the generation of physical reality from a single source. It is not merely a list of gods, but a systematic description of how the Monad (Atum) differentiated into the complexity of the material world.

Atum: The Monad

The "Complete One" who brought himself into being within Nun through Heka. He represents pure potentiality before manifestation.

Shu & Tefnut

The first duality: Shu (Void/Air) and Tefnut (Moisture/Order). They represent the first space required for creation to exist.

Geb & Nut

The horizontal and vertical axes: Geb (Earth) and Nut (Sky). Their separation by Shu allowed for the beginning of time.

The Ennead is completed by the four children of Geb and Nut: Osiris, Isis, Set, and Nephthys. These deities represent the entry of divine power into the social and moral realms of humanity—the introduction of death, magic, kingship, and the struggle between order and chaos.

The Ogdoad (Hermopolis)

The Ogdoad (Greek ogdoás, "eight") represents the primordial state of the universe before the first sunrise. While the Ennead describes creation, the Ogdoad describes the pre-conditions of creation. These eight deities represent the four qualities of the infinite void:

Nu & Naunet

Depth: The primordial, inert waters of the abyss.

Heh & Hauhet

Infinity: The lack of boundaries or spatial limits.

Kek & Kauket

Darkness: The absolute absence of light or perception.

Amun & Amaunet

Hiddenness: The invisible, omnipresent spirit that triggers the collapse into reality.

According to Hermopolitan theology, these eight forces eventually converged at Khemenu to create the "Cosmic Egg" (or the Primordial Lotus), from which the sun god emerged to begin the first day. This event marked the transition from Isfet (unstructured potential) to Ma'at (structured reality).
