The Ultimate Adversary

Apep, known as Apophis in Greek, is the embodiment of Isfet (primordial chaos). Depicted as a giant serpent, he exists in the darkness of the underworld (Duat) and seeks to swallow the sun god Ra each night as he travels through the twelve hours of darkness.

Unlike Set, who is a complex deity representing necessary chaos within the system of Ma'at, Apep represents the total destruction of the system itself—the return to the non-existence (non-being) of the primordial void before creation.

The Battle of Ra's Barque

Each night, Ra's solar barque must navigate the Duat. Apep uses his hypnotic gaze to paralyze the crew, but the god Set, standing at the prow of the boat, is the only one strong enough to resist the serpent's power and spear it, allowing the sun to rise again.

The Book of Overthrowing Apep

A major collection of ritual spells designed to magically weaken the serpent. These rituals involved creating wax models of Apep, spitting on them, and burning them—a form of sympathetic magic intended to ensure the daily victory of Ma'at over Isfet.

Symbolism and Entropy

In modern esoteric interpretation, Apep can be understood as the principle of entropy—the tendency of all organized systems to dissolve into disorder. The daily struggle against Apep represents the constant effort required to maintain consciousness and order in the face of the encroaching unconsciousness and void.
