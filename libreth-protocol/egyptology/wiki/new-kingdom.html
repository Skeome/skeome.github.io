The Imperial Era (c. 1550–1075 BCE)

Inaugurated by the expulsion of the Hyksos, the New Kingdom was the era of Egypt's greatest imperial power and artistic achievement. The cult of Amun-Ra became the dominant state theology, and temple construction at Karnak and Luxor reached its peak.

The Book of the Dead

The formalization of the Reu nu pert em hru (Book of the Dead) occurred during this period. This collection of over 200 spells served as a comprehensive manual for the soul's successful passage through the Hall of Judgment and its integration into the Netherworld.

The Heretical Break

The 18th Dynasty was interrupted by the Amarna Period, where Akhenaten attempted to center all divinity on the Aten. The subsequent restoration returned Egypt to its traditional polytheistic framework under Tutankhamun and later the Ramesside pharaohs.
