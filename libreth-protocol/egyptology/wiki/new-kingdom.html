I. The Ontology of Empire — The Warrior-God

The New Kingdom (c. 1550–1075 BCE) represents the absolute peak of Egyptian geopolitical power and theological complexity. After the trauma of the Hyksos occupation, the Pharaonic state transformed itself into an expansive, military-oriented empire.

In the framework of the Libreth Protocol, this is the High-Bandwidth Expansion Phase. The Pharaonic ideal shifted from the "Shepherd-King" of the Middle Kingdom to the "Warrior-God," who actively pushed back the borders of Isfet through military conquest and the construction of colossal monumental resonators (temples).

II. The 18th Dynasty — Thutmosid Expansion

HATSHEPSUT

One of the most effective female Pharaohs, she emphasized trade and divine legitimacy. Her mortuary temple at Deir el-Bahari is a masterpiece of architectural geometry, designed to align her reign with the primordial cycles of Hathor.

THUTMOSE III

The "Napoleon of Egypt," he conducted 17 military campaigns, cementing control over the Levant and Nubia. His reign established the Karnak temple complex as the spiritual and economic engine of the world.

V. The Priesthood of Amun-Ra

The New Kingdom was defined by the supremacy of Amun-Ra, the "King of the Gods." The High Priests of Amun at Thebes eventually controlled more land and resources than the Pharaoh himself.

This institutional density permitted the development of the most sophisticated magical and theological systems in history, including the Netherworld Books and the finalized Book of the Dead. However, the resulting power struggle between the Throne and the Temple eventually led to the systemic collapse of the 20th Dynasty.

VI. The Valley of the Kings — Hidden Technology

Abandoning the visible pyramids of the Old Kingdom, the New Kingdom Pharaohs hid their tombs deep within the limestone cliffs of the Biban el-Moluk.

This was a theological shift: the tomb was no longer a solar ladder, but a re-entry node into the chthonic depths of the Duat. The walls of these tombs were covered in functional magical diagrams designed to permit the King's Ba to navigate the 12 hours of the night and emerge as a transfigured Akh at dawn.
