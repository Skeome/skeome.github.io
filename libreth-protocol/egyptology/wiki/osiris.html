OSIRIS [Asar]

// LORD OF THE AFTERLIFE AND RESURRECTION //

Wiki Home > Pantheon > Osiris

The First to Return

Osiris (Asar) is the god of death, resurrection, and the fertile silt of the Nile. He was the first pharaoh to die and be returned to life through the magic of Isis, establishing the ontological prototype for all human resurrection.

The Green Man (Wadj-Wer)

Osiris is often depicted with green skin, symbolizing his role as the god of vegetation and the annual renewal of life. He is the life force that persists through winter/death to emerge in the spring.

The Corn Osiris

A specific ritual where grain was planted in a mold shaped like Osiris. The sprouting grain symbolized the god's resurrection within the earth, linking human survival to agricultural fertility.

The Lord of Silence (Neb-Ger)

As the judge of the dead, Osiris is the Lord of Silence. He presides over the Hall of Two Truths in absolute stillness. This silence is not empty; it is the perfect stability of Ma'at after the storm of existence. He determines which souls may transfigure into the immortal Akh.

Alchemical Correspondence

In alchemical traditions, Osiris is associated with the Nigredo (Blackening) stage. His dismemberment by Set represents the necessary dissolution of the old state before the creation of the Philosopher's Stone. The reconstitution of his body is the supreme image of the "Great Work."
