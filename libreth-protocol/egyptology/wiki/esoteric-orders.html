The Survival of the Mysteries

The 17th-century emergence of modern initiatory societies saw a deliberate revival of Hermetic and Egyptian themes, viewing these ancient traditions as the source of a "Secret Wisdom" preserved through the ages.

Rosicrucianism

The Rosicrucian manifestos claimed the existence of a secret brotherhood possessing ancient Egyptian-Hermetic wisdom. Their program for a "Total Reform of Learning" was based on the synthesis of Christian theology with Hermetic natural philosophy and Alchemy.

Freemasonry

Speculative Freemasonry incorporated extensive Egyptian symbolism into its rituals and architecture. Key elements include:

  • The All-Seeing Eye: Resonance with the Eye of Horus (Wedjat).
  • The Pyramid: Symbol of stability and the primordial mound.
  • Initiatory Rites: Rituals modeled on death and resurrection, mirroring the Osirian mythos and Hermetic palingenesia.
  • Hiram Abiff: The master builder, often linked to the craftsman-god Ptah.
