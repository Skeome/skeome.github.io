The Rosetta Stone breakthrough

The ability to read Hieroglyphs was lost by the 4th century CE, leading to centuries of speculation that the signs were purely allegorical or mystical symbols. The 1799 discovery of the Rosetta Stone—featuring a decree in Hieroglyphic, Demotic, and Ancient Greek—provided the key.

Key Figures

  • Thomas Young: Identified that cartouches contained royal names and realized that some signs were phonetic.
  • Jean-François Champollion: Through his mastery of Coptic, he realized that hieroglyphs were a complex combination of phonetic signs and ideograms. His 1822 letter to the Académie des Inscriptions marks the birth of modern Egyptology.

The Role of Coptic

Champollion's breakthrough was his realization that the Coptic language was the final stage of the ancient Egyptian tongue. This connection allowed him to bridge the gap between the unknown glyphs and a living voice, enabling the translation of thousands of years of recorded history.
