V. Coptic as the Resonant Remnant — Calibration of Vowels

The primary source for reconstructing Egyptian vowels is Coptic, the final stage of the language, which used the Greek alphabet and included vowels.

In our framework, Coptic serves as the Resonant Remnant. By tracing sound shifts backward, phonologists can estimate the vocalic structure of earlier periods. For example, the New Kingdom name of Aset likely sounded closer to *ʔŪsat. By the Coptic period, this had shifted to Ēse. This progression allows the modern researcher to "Tune" their pronunciation to the specific historical epoch they wish to resonate with. See Coptic: The Final Form Wiki.
