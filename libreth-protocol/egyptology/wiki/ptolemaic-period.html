The Alexandrian Synthesis (332–30 BCE)

Following the conquest of Egypt by Alexander the Great, the Ptolemaic dynasty created a unique environment where Kemetic and Hellenic intellectual traditions intersected. Alexandria became the global center for astronomy, geometry, and medicine.

Birth of the Hermetic Tradition

This syncretism gave rise to Hermeticism, centered on the figure of Hermes Trismegistus—a fusion of the Egyptian Thoth and the Greek Hermes. Initiates sought direct spiritual experience (Gnosis) of the divine through the study of the cosmos.

Temple Construction and Codification

The Ptolemies aggressively patronized traditional religion, sponsoring the construction of massive temple complexes at Dendera, Edfu, Philae, and Kom Ombo. These temples contain some of the most detailed religious inscriptions in history, as the priesthood engaged in a systematic project of codifying ancient wisdom.

It was during this period that the Greek Magical Papyri (PGM) began to emerge, reflecting the vibrant, multilingual practice of everyday Heka.
