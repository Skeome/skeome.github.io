The Earth and Sky: Geb and Nut

The fundamental duality of the physical universe is represented by the siblings Geb (Earth) and Nut (Sky). In Egyptian art, Nut is shown arched over Geb, her body covered in stars, representing the vault of heaven, while Geb lies below, symbolizing the fertile land.

Nut: The Celestial Mother

Nut swallows the sun each evening and births it each morning, a cycle that directly parallels the Solar Barque’s journey. She is the cosmic container of time and reality.

Geb: The Great Cackler

The earth god, whose laughter was believed to cause earthquakes. He is the material foundation and the seat of the throne of Egypt.

The Moment of Creation: The Benben

The transition from the unstructured potential of Nun to the ordered world began with the emergence of the Benben—the primordial mound. This event was signaled by the arrival of the Benu Bird (the Egyptian phoenix), who alighted upon the mound and let out a primary cry, the cosmic vibration that initiated the beginning of Time (Zep Tepi).

Khepri: The Principle of Becoming

Represented by the scarab beetle, Khepri is the morning aspect of the sun god. He embodies the principle of Kheper—perpetual transformation and self-generation. He is the active force that rolls the sun across the awareness field, ensuring that reality is never static but always in a state of creative evolution.
