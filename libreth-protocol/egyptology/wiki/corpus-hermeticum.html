The Wisdom of Alexandria

The Corpus Hermeticum is a collection of 17 Greek treatises, primarily dialogues between Hermes Trismegistus and his disciples (Tat, Asclepius) or the divine Nous (Mind). They represent the philosophical core of the Hermetic tradition.

CH I: POIMANDRES

The foundational vision. Hermes receives a revelation from the "Shepherd of Men" (Nous) concerning the creation of the cosmos, the fall of Man into matter, and the path of return through the planetary spheres.

CH XIII: THE SECRET DISCOURSE

The most initiatory text. It describes the process of "rebirth" (palingenesia) in which the old self is dismantled and rebuilt through the infusion of the "ten powers of God," mirroring Egyptian funerary transfiguration.

NHC VI,6: THE DISCOURSE ON THE EIGHTH AND NINTH

A Coptic text from the Nag Hammadi library. It provides the clearest evidence of actual initiatory practice, describing an ascent through the spheres using vowel chants.

Authenticity and Origins

While once believed to be from pre-dynastic Egypt, philological analysis by Isaac Casaubon in 1614 proved they were written in the early centuries CE. However, modern scholarship confirms they authentically reflect the initiatory traditions of the Egyptian priesthood (House of Life) adapted for a Hellenistic audience.
