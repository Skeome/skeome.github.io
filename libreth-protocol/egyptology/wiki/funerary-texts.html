I. The Ontology of Funerary Texts — Functional Heka

The Egyptian funerary texts were not merely religious prayers or commemorative inscriptions. They were functional magical technologies (Heka) designed to ensure the survival of the individual's composite soul and its transfiguration into an Akh (a radiant, immortal spirit).

In the framework of the Libreth Protocol, these texts are viewed as navigational software for the awareness field. They provided the passwords, identifications, and frequencies required to traverse the 12 hours of the night and successfully navigate the threshold of judgment.

II. Pyramid Texts — The Royal Celestial Ascent

OLD KINGDOM (c. 2350–2181 BCE)

Inscribed on the interior walls of 5th and 6th Dynasty pyramids, these are the oldest religious texts in the world. They were royally exclusive and focused on the King's celestial ascent. The goal was for the King to join the "Imperishable Stars" (the circumpolar stars) and achieve unity with the creator god Atum-Re.

III. Coffin Texts — The Democratization of the Afterlife

MIDDLE KINGDOM (c. 2055–1650 BCE)

As central authority decentralized, the royal funerary privileges were adopted by the provincial elites. Spells were inscribed on the interior of wooden coffins. This era introduced the Book of Two Ways, the first illustrated map of the underworld, detailing the paths of fire and water the soul must navigate.

IV. The Book of the Dead — Going Forth by Day

NEW KINGDOM (c. 1550–1070 BCE)

Known to the Egyptians as the Pert em Heru ("The Spells for Coming Forth by Day"), these were written on papyrus scrolls placed within the tomb. They were standardized yet customizable, featuring "rubrics" (instructions in red ink) and vignettes (illustrations) that served as visual resonators for the magic.

V. Spell 125 — The Hall of Two Truths

The most critical component of the New Kingdom funerary technology is Spell 125. It details the soul's arrival in the Hall of Two Truths, where the heart (Ib) is weighed against the feather of Ma'at.

The deceased must perform the "Negative Confession," declaring innocence of 42 specific transgressions before a tribunal of 42 divine assessors. Success in this trial resulted in the state of Ma'at Kheru ("True of Voice"), permitting the soul's final transfiguration into light.
