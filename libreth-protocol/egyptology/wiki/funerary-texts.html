The Evolution of Protection

The Egyptian funerary texts were not merely religious prayers but functional magical technologies (Heka) designed to ensure the soul's survival and its transfiguration into an Akh (radiant spirit).

PYRAMID TEXTS (Old Kingdom)

The oldest known body of religious literature. Inscribed on the walls of royal tombs to facilitate the pharaoh's ascent to the stars. They describe the king's transformation into a falcon or an "imperishable star."

COFFIN TEXTS (Middle Kingdom)

The "democratization of the afterlife." Spells previously reserved for royalty were adapted for local elites and inscribed on wooden coffins. Includes the Book of Two Ways, the first map of the underworld.

BOOK OF THE DEAD (New Kingdom)

Properly titled Reu nu pert em hru ("Spells for Going Forth by Day"). A collection of over 200 individual spells, including Spell 125: The Negative Confession and Weighing of the Heart.

Spell 125: The Hall of Two Truths

The most critical trial for the deceased. The soul must declare innocence of 42 specific sins before divine assessors. Thoth records the result as the heart is weighed against the feather of Ma'at. If successful, the soul is declared maat-kheru (true of voice).
