Reunification and Refinement (c. 2055–1650 BCE)

The Middle Kingdom is often considered the classical age of Egyptian culture and language. This era saw the rise of the god Amun-Ra to prominence and the perfection of Middle Egyptian literature, such as the Tale of Sinuhe.

Expansion of the Coffin Texts

Building on the decentralization of the First Intermediate Period, the Middle Kingdom saw a vast expansion of the Coffin Texts. These 1,185 known spells provided individuals with the magical protection needed to navigate the Duat.

Spell 1130: One of the most significant spells, where the creator god Ra addresses all humanity, declaring that he created the "four winds" for every person—an early expression of cosmic equity.

The Book of Two Ways

Often painted on the floors of Middle Kingdom coffins, the Book of Two Ways provides the first known map of the underworld, offering routes by water or land and requiring the deceased to know the names of various gatekeepers.
