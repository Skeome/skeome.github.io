Reunification and Refinement (c. 2055–1650 BCE)

The Middle Kingdom began when Mentuhotep II of the 11th Dynasty defeated the Herakleopolitan kings of the north, reunifying Egypt under Theban rule. He was depicted wearing the Red Crown of Lower Egypt and the White Crown of Upper Egypt, signaling the restoration of the "Two Lands."

Propaganda & Literature

This era produced sophisticated political literature like the Prophecy of Neferti, which retroactively predicted the chaos of the intermediate period and the coming of a savior king (Amenemhat I) to restore order.

The Rise of Amun

The obscure local god Amun ("The Hidden One") was elevated to the state pantheon, eventually merging with the sun god to become Amun-Ra, King of the Gods. This theological move synthesized the invisible, omnipresent power of wind/breath (Amun) with the visible, life-giving power of the sun (Ra).

The Coffin Texts: Spell 1130

The Middle Kingdom's greatest theological contribution was the Coffin Texts. Spell 1130 contains a revolutionary declaration by the creator god:

"I made the four winds that every man might breathe... I made every man like his fellow; and I did not command that they do evil. It is their hearts that violate what I have said."

This text asserts moral responsibility and the fundamental equality of human access to the divine breath.

The Book of Two Ways

Often painted on the floors of Middle Kingdom coffins, the Book of Two Ways provides the first known map of the underworld, offering routes by water or land and requiring the deceased to know the names of various gatekeepers.
