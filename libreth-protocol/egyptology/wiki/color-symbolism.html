Overview

In Ancient Egypt, color was not merely an aesthetic choice but a theological language. The term iwn could refer to "color," "nature," "being," or "disposition." Each color possessed a specific vibration and was associated with particular deities, cosmological concepts, and magical properties.

Black (Kem)

Symbol of rebirth and fertility. It represents the rich silt of the Nile and is associated with Osiris (as the "Black One") and Anubis. Mineral: Galena or Carbon. It is the frequency of the "hidden sun" during the twelve hours of night.

Red (Desher)

Associated with danger, power, and Set. Mineral: Red Jasper or Carnelian. It represents the dynamic, chaotic energy required for protection and the destruction of enemies. It is the frequency of the desert sun.

White (Hedj)

Symbol of purity and holiness. Mineral: Silver or Limestone. Used for ritual tools and priestly garments. It represents the clarified state of the soul after the Opening of the Mouth.

Green (Wadj)

Symbol of vegetation, growth, and eternal life. Mineral: Malachite. Associated with Osiris as the god of renewal. It represents the "flourishing" of the spirit within the Awareness Field.

Blue (Irtyu)

Symbol of the sky and divinity. Mineral: Lapis Lazuli. Deep blue represents the night sky and the Milky Way; it is the frequency of the celestial waters from which creation emerges.

Yellow/Gold (Khenet)

The "flesh of the gods." Material: Gold. Symbol of immortality and the unchanging nature of divine beings. It represents the perfected, incorruptible state of the transfigured Akh.

Application in Heka

The choice of color for amulets and ritual implements was critical, as the material's color governed its metaphysical resonance. A red carnelian amulet was used to channel the dynamic energy of Set, while a green faience scarab was used to attract the restorative power of Osiris.
