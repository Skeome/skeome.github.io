The Practice of Sacred Sleep

In Ancient Egypt, dreams were considered a primary means of communication between the gods and humanity. The practice of Dream Incubation (Ancient Greek koimēsis) involved sleeping within the sacred precincts of a temple specifically to receive a divine dream—usually for healing or oracular guidance.

Temples dedicated to deities like Imhotep (the deified architect and physician) and Thoth were popular sites for this practice, particularly during the Late and Ptolemaic periods.

The Ritual of Incubation

The incubation process was not merely about sleeping; it involved a series of ritualized steps:

Purification

Abstinence from certain foods and physical purification (bathing) before entering the sacred space.

Petition

The individual would present their question or request to the deity, often accompanied by offerings or written petitions.

The Sleep

Sleeping on a special bed or mat in a designated area of the temple, often in the vicinity of a statue of the deity.

Upon waking, the individual would consult with temple priests who specialized in the interpretation of dreams (Ancient Egyptian ḥry-sšṯꜣ, "Master of Secrets").

Oneiromancy: The Art of Interpretation

The interpretation of dreams, or oneiromancy, was a highly specialized scribal skill within the House of Life. A key primary source for this practice is the Chester Beatty Dream Papyrus (dating from the 19th Dynasty), which contains a systematic list of dream images and their corresponding meanings, categorized as either "good" or "bad."

The Sau Priest

The sau (magician-priest) served as the primary interpreter of dreams. Their role was to decode the symbolic language of the divine and provide practical guidance or defensive rituals to counteract malevolent omens discovered during the sacred sleep.

The Serapeum and Late Period Practice

The Serapeum at Memphis became the premier site for dream incubation during the Late and Ptolemaic periods. This massive underground complex, dedicated to the syncretic deity Serapis (a fusion of Osiris and Apis), attracted pilgrims from across the Mediterranean who sought healing and oracular revelations through the "sacred sleep" in its subterranean chambers.
