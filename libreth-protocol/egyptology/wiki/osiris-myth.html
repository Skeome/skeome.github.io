Betrayal and Dismemberment

The Osiris myth cycle details the reign of Osiris, a benevolent king who brought civilization to Kemet, and his subsequent betrayal by his brother Set. Set's murder of Osiris and the scattering of his body across the land represented a total collapse of Ma'at.

The Quest of Isis

Isis, the wife of Osiris and a powerful magician, embarked on a quest to recover the pieces of her husband. With the assistance of Nephthys, Thoth, and Anubis, she reassembled the body and performed the first rites of mummification. Through her spoken words of power (Heka), she was able to breathe life into Osiris long enough to conceive their son, Horus.

The Prototype of Resurrection

This myth established the prototype for the "Opening of the Mouth" ceremony and the belief that every deceased person could become an "Osiris" through proper ritual intervention. It provides the narrative framework for the Funerary Corpus and the alchemical stages of Nigredo and Albedo.
