VI. The Crux Ansata — The Ankh as Christian Symbol

The Ankh (ꜥnḫ [𓋹]), the hieroglyphic sign for "Life," was adopted by early Egyptian Christians as the Crux Ansata (the "Handled Cross").

In our research framework, this was a Symbolic System-Merge. The Ankh appeared prominently on Coptic textiles, stelae, and in church architecture, acting as a bridge that allowed the ancestral understanding of Eternal Life to flow into the new religious framework.
