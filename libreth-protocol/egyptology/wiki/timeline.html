I. The Ontology of Time — History as Sacred Vessel

Ancient Egyptian civilization did not separate history from theology. Every reign was viewed as a repetition of cosmic archetypes; every Pharaoh was Horus in life and Osiris in death. To understand Kemetic esoteric traditions, one must first inhabit its timeline — not merely as a sequence of dates, but as a series of initiatory cycles.

In the framework of the Libreth Protocol, the timeline is viewed as a continuous data-stream that undergoes periodic phases of initialization, expansion, decoherence, and restoration. Each "Intermediate Period" is a system stress-test, while each "Kingdom" is a stable state crystallization of Ma'at.
