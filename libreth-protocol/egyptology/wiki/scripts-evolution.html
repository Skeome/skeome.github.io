The Medu Netjer and its Cursives

The Egyptian language was recorded through several distinct script systems over its 3,500-year history. While Hieroglyphs remained the sacred monumental script, cursive forms evolved for administrative and daily use.

Script Period Primary Function
Hieroglyphic 3100 BCE – 394 CE Monumental inscriptions, sacred texts. "Divine Words."
Hieratic 3100 BCE – 700 CE Administrative, literary, and religious texts. Scribes' shorthand.
Demotic 650 BCE – 452 CE Legal, commercial, and scientific texts. The everyday script.
Coptic 200 CE – Present Christian religious texts. Greek alphabet + 7 demotic signs.

Coptic: The Bridge to Sound

Coptic is the final stage of the Egyptian language and the only stage that preserves vowels systematically. This makes it invaluable for reconstructing the pronunciation of hieroglyphic texts. Coptic Christianity in the Egyptian desert (the Desert Fathers) reflects a transformation of earlier Egyptian temple spirituality into a new initiatory context.
