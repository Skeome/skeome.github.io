VIII. JSesh — The Professional Scribe’s Workbench

JSesh is the industry-standard word processor for hieroglyphic texts. Developed by Serge Rosmorduc, it allows for the precise transcription of Medu Netjer using Gardiner Sign Codes.

In this archive, JSesh is the primary tool for Encoding the Word. It allows the modern scribe to manipulate the signs with the same precision as the ancient painters of Deir el-Medina. IX. Fabricius (Google) — Machine Learning in Epigraphy

Fabricius is an interactive workbench developed by Google Arts & Culture that utilizes Machine Learning to assist in the decoding and facsimile-creation of hieroglyphs.
