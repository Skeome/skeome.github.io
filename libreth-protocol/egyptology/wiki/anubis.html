The Jackal God: Pre-Osirian Supremacy

Anubis, or Inpu, is the jackal-headed deity associated with mummification and the afterlife. Before the rise of Osiris in the Middle Kingdom, Anubis was the undisputed Lord of the Dead. He held the supreme title of Khenty-Imentiu ("Foremost of the Westerners"), a name that would later be absorbed by Osiris as the latter's funerary influence grew.

His black color (not found in actual jackals) symbolizes the fertile soil of the Nile and the promise of rebirth from decay, marking him as a god of transition and regeneration rather than mere death.

The Inventor of Embalming

Anubis is credited as the first to develop the science of mummification. In the foundational myth cycle, it was Anubis who reassembled the body of the murdered Osiris and performed the first rites of preservation, allowing the god's spirit to be restored. This act established the template for all subsequent Egyptian funerary practices.

Master of Secrets (Hery-Shesheta)

Anubis is the keeper of the secret knowledge required for the preservation of the body and the navigation of the spirit world. He oversaw the "Weryt"—the place of embalming.

Wepwawet: The Opener of Ways

Often conflated with Anubis, Wepwawet is a distinct lupine deity of Upper Egypt. While Anubis guards the dead, Wepwawet "opens the paths" for the sun barque and the deceased to enter the Duat.

The Weighing of the Heart and the PGM

In the Weighing of the Heart ceremony, Anubis is the one who leads the deceased to the scales and ensures the accuracy of the judgment. As the "Guardian of the Scales," he is the ultimate arbiter of the soul's integrity.

His influence persisted well into late antiquity, appearing prominently in the Greek Magical Papyri (PGM). In these texts, Anubis is invoked as a powerful assistant (paredros) who can fetch spirits, reveal hidden truths, and act as a bridge between the magician and the celestial realms.
