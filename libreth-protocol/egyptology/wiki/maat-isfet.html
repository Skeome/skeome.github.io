I. The Ontology of the Polarity — Order vs. Entropy

Ancient Egyptian theology is organized around the fundamental dynamic between Ma'at (Order) and Isfet (Chaos). This is not a simple good-vs-evil dualism, but a sophisticated model of cosmic maintenance.

In the framework of the Libreth Protocol, Ma'at represents coherence — the sustained effort required to keep information organized and functional. Isfet represents entropy — the natural tendency of all systems to dissolve into noise and primordial potential. Reality is the state of tension between these two forces.

II. Ma'at — Truth, Balance, and Cosmic Law

Ma'at is simultaneously a goddess, an abstract principle, and a practical ethical standard. She is the daughter of Re and the wife of Thoth, representing the inherent intelligibility of the universe.

THE FEATHER OF TRUTH

Ma'at is personified by the ostrich feather. It represents the perfect bilateral symmetry of the cosmos and the lightness required of the heart to survive the judgment of Osiris.

THE FIVE DIMENSIONS

Ma'at governs: (1) Cosmic order (the sun's path), (2) Truth in speech, (3) Justice in law, (4) Ethical conduct, and (5) Ritual correctness.

III. Isfet — Primordial Chaos and Dissolution

Isfet is the uncreated state. It existed before the first sunrise and surrounds the ordered world on all sides. It is personified by the serpent Apep, who attempts to swallow the sun each night.

Isfet is not to be destroyed (which is impossible) but to be contained and directed. The controlled irruption of Isfet — such as the annual Nile flood — was recognized as a necessary source of renewal for the ordered world.

V. The Hall of Judgment — The Final Accounting

The climax of the soul's journey is the Weighing of the Heart. The heart (Ib), representing the individual's cumulative ethical frequency, is weighed against the feather of Ma'at.

A heart that is "True of Voice" (Ma'at Kheru) is light enough to permit the soul's transfiguration into an Akh. A heart heavy with Isfet is consumed by Ammit, resulting in the "Second Death" — total annihilation of the individual data-stream.
