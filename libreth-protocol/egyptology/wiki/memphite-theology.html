The Shabaka Stone

The Memphite Theology is preserved on the Shabaka Stone (c. 710 BCE). It records the creation of the universe by the god Ptah, the patron of craftsmen. Ptah is described as creating all things through his "heart" (thought) and his "tongue" (speech).

Sia, Hu, and Heka

This narrative is the primary source for the creative triad:

  • Sia: Divine Perception (Heart). The conception of the idea.
  • Hu: Authoritative Utterance (Tongue). The naming of the idea.
  • Heka: The vital power that enables the transition from thought to reality.

This framework directly informs the Hermetic concept of the Logos and the role of the Nous.

Ptah as the Master Architect

Ptah represents the divine intellect manifesting as material form. He is the craftsman of the gods, providing the "signature" (Σ) for all created beings.
